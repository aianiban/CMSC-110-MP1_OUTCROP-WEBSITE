About 400 jeepney drivers and operators of Baguio - Benguet participated in the nationwide transportation strike against Memorandum Circular 2019-013, which aims to phase-out old jeepney models by PUV modernization, September 30.

The protest, led by Pinagkaisang Samahan ng mga Tsuper at Operators Nationwide (PISTON), began with a march from Sunshine Park to Igorot Park, where different jeepney drivers' associations voiced out their concerns regarding the impending modernization and phase out to be implemented after nine months.

Josefino Bautista, Treasurer of BIPSIHODA, said that PUV Modernization is anti-poor. Should the phase out be executed, it would affect not only the jeepney drivers and operators but also citizens who commute daily as the modernization would entail a fare hike.

"Kaya ako tumututol sa sinasabi nila dahil tayo, bawat isang operator ay aalisan ng karapatan na magtrabaho dito sa sariling bayan na magbigay ng karangyaan at mababang pasahe sa mga mahihirap na ating kababayan," Bautista stated.

The program ended with a community dance as a call for action to stop the phase-out and PUV modernization.

In commemoration of the declaration of Martial Law 47 years ago, students of the University of the Philippines Baguio, along with progressive groups in Benguet, and citizens from different sectors protest on September 20 as they marched from UP Baguio to Igorot Park.

Students of UP Baguio started the event by conducting a snake rally within the campus, in line with the observance of the systemwide UP Day of Remembrance.

UP Rises Against Tyranny and Dictatorship - UP Baguio and its member organizations along with Tongtongan Ti Umili, Cordillera Peoples Alliance, and Cordillera Human Rights Alliance led the March for Justice and Democracy with the theme: "Laban Kabataan, Laban Amianan." Mikhaila Sarita, Anakbayan Metro Baguio Secretary-General, condemned the Martial Law that happened 47 years ago as well as the injustices happening in the current administration in the form of de facto martial law.

"Sa paggunita natin sa pagpataw ni Marcos ng Martial Law sa Pilipinas, ang panawagan natin ay hindi na never again. Dahil nandito na ang martial law, kitang kita natin dahil sa dami ng mga pinatay at kinulong dahil lamang sila ay lumalaban," she stated.

Meanwhile, Gabriel Siscar of UP RISE encouraged everyone, especially the youth, to be critical citizens and continue to battle against the evident injustices by the current administration.

Nico Ponce, Chairperson of UP Baguio University Student Council, also raised the significance of the youth's collective actions in condemning the de facto martial law.

"Walang mali sa ginagawa ng mga iskolar ng bayan. Makatwiran ang maghimagsik, makatwiran ang paglaban, ang estado ang dahilan kung bakit kami naririto ngayon," Ponce stated.

On the other hand, Julius Baquiran, Chairperson of Anakbayan Metro Baguio, highlighted in his statement our need to fight for our absolute democracy.

"Hangga't may patayan, hangga't may tiraniya, hangga't may pasismo, tuloy tuloy tayong maghihimagsik. Tuloy tuloy tayong lalaban at makikibaka," he said.

The program ended with the crowd chanting "Bayan, Bayan, Bayan Ko" led by Baquiran which signified the unity for justice and democracy.

UPB Freshies nakiisa sa mga kilos-protesta

Nasubok ang pakikiisa ng 642 na mga bagong iskolar ng bayan ng Unibersidad ng Pilipinas Baguio (UPB) sa pagtindig laban sa mga isyung kinahaharap ng bayan sa sunod-sunod na mga kilos-protesta sa kanilang unang linggo sa unibersidad.

Sa naganap na Freshman Welcome Orientation sa bagong gawang Himnasyo Amianan noong ika-5 ng Agosto, pinangunahan ni Nationalist Corps Head Perine Agraviador ang lightning rally kung saan hinamon niya ang mga freshman na tumindig para sa bayan.

Nabigyang diin din ang Memorandum Order No. 32 na naglalayong magtalaga ng mga dagdag na tauhan ng Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP) sa Bicol, Samar, at Isla ng Negros upang bawasan ang bilang ng mga karahasan na di umano ay gawa ng mga â€œlawless groups.â€

Naglunsad din ng kilos-protesta sa kantina ng UPB sa unang araw ng mga bagong iskolar ng bayan bilang pakikiisa sa malawakang First Day Fight sa buong UP System upang sila ay himukin sa paglaban sa mga isyung kinakaharap ng bansa.

Dagdag pa rito, naging mainit din ang pagsalubong sa unang linggo ng mga bagong iskolar ng bayan sa UPB matapos ang insidente ng pagbaril sa paralegal volunteer ng Cordillera Human Rights Alliance (CHRA) at Provincial Human Rights Officer ng Ifugao na si Brandon Lee noong ika-6 ng Agosto.

Baguio City celebrates Pride March 2018 With the theme â€œThis is Prideâ€, various LGBTQ+ organizations from Baguio-Benguet community came together to wave the rainbow flag and to celebrate the annual Pride March at Igorot Park, Baguio City on November 24, 2018.

SOGIE Equality Bill protects not just LGBTQ+ members but everyone from discriminatory acts such as denial of access to public services, including SOGIE as a criteria for hiring or dismissal of workers, refusing admission or expelling students in schools based on SOGIE, engaging in public speech which intends to shame or ridicule LGBTQ+ persons, harassment based on SOGIE and the likes.
