Filipinos stand firm in regaining control over West Philippine Sea
// Isagani M. Caspe

China has continued to terrorize our countrymen and seeks to endanger further our sovereignty but Filipinos stand against the domination of our sea.

The United Nation Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS) acknowledges the fact hat Scarborough Shoal falls within the 200-nautical-mile Exclusive Economic Zone (EEC) of the Philippines. Clearly, Filipinos hold the sole rights to all the maritime resources of the island.

However, since the standoff incident between the Chinese and Philippine vessels in Scarborough Shoal in 2012, China dominated West Philippine Sea. They started to develop bases on the island, deploy fighter jets to other military facilities they constructed, built bunkers, shelters, radar and signalling systems that still operate until today.

Few months after the standoff incident, agreement had been done for both countries to pull out. The Philippines pulled out, China did not. They stayed in our sea and ended up in control.

In January 2013, former President Benigno Aquino decided to file a case against them. In 2016, the Permanent Court of Arbitration stated that China was no legal basis to claim the rights to resources in the sea areas falling within the nine-dash line.

But China’s arbitral ruling is unshakeable. They reiterated that part of their Exclusive Economic Zone (EEC) is the West Philippine Sea; hence, they will not recognize the result of the arbitration.

Then, in 2016, there was a bilateral agreement between China and the Philippines under the new administration of President Rodrigo Duterte. This gave the Filipino fishermen the “imaginary” freedom to benefit from the maritime resources in the West Philippine Sea, with theChina coast guards patrolling the water.

When Rodrigo Duterte assumed as the President of the Philippines, he established a strong political bond with China, the one who used and abused our own maritime resources.

Despite having friendly ties with Chinese government, stories of exploitation not just in our resources but also to our people remain.

According to fishermen in Masinloc Zambales, the privilege given by the agreement does not really apply in Scarborough Shoal, because down there none of those matters – Chinese rule.

They are also prohibited to use large boats inside thelagoon of the Island, only small boats can fish inside because at the entrance of the island, vessels of China are guarding.

Fishermen in Scarborough Shoal reported that the Chinese coast guards boarded their boat, opened their storage, take the best of their catch without permission, then they leave as soon as they have their fill.

Recently, China violated Article 98 of the United Nation Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS) which directs that, every state is required to render assistance to any person in distress or in danger of being lost at sea.

They abandoned 22 Filipino seafarers in the Reed Bankafter the Chinese and Philippine vessels’ collision incident.

MO 32 implemetation escalates human rights violation
// Mecy Siteg

Contrary to lawless violence suppression, the implementation of Memorandum Order No. 32 has resulted to the spike of human rights abuses and relentless attacks towards human rights defenders.

The MO 32 was issued last November 22, deploying additional forces from the Armed Forces of the Philippines and the Philippine National Police to suppress lawlessness violence and acts of terror in Samar, Negros Occidental, Negros Oriental and Bicol.

The Kilusang Magbubukid ng Pilipinas, however, warned that MO 32 will only give further authority to the AFP and PNP to carry out red-tagging of activists, illegal arrests and detentions and military encampment in communities in the said provinces.

The National Federation of Peasant Women (Amihan) also reported that the deployment of nine army battalions in Eastern Visayas affected 545 villages forcing evacuations in San Jorge, Calbiga, Las Navas, San Jose De Buan and Pinabacdao including aerial bombings in Matuginao and Las Navas.

In addition, the military operations of the 87 th Infantry Battalion of the 8 th Infantry Division of the Philippine Army have forcibly evacuated 1,409 from their communities in Barangays Hagbay, San Nicolas, Can-ponte and San Pedro in San Juan de Buan, Samar on March 8 and 17 to 26.

Eleven regular and special battalions are also operating in Negros and at least 68 were killed and more than 70 were illegally arrested and detained while 62 individuals were killed in Bicol region including human rights activists’ Bagasala, Hubilla and Morada.

The National Democratic Front of the Philippines also criticized MO 32 and said that it is President Duterte’s ground work for nationwide martial law to prolong his cling to power while the Alliance for the Advancement of People’s Rights (KARAPATAN) stated that it is an additional directive to kill and militarize targeted communities.

However, Department of National Defense Secretary Delfin Lorenzana and DILG Secretary Eduardo Año both dismissed claims that MO 32 will be the prelude for declaring nationwide martial law while the AFP and PNP also said that they do not have the intention to support its imposition.

Conversely, Senator Leila de Lima condemned the killings of human rights workers and said that the continued harassment against President Duterte’s critics is aimed at silencing those who bravely stand up against the oppressive acts of the current administration and also stressed that Filipinos should not remain blind and deaf to the plight of their fellow countrymen who lost their loved ones to Duterte’s bloody war against illegal drugs and the rampant abuse of power under the current administration.

Executive Secretary Salvador Medialdea signed MO 32 upon the order of President Duterte amid the "sporadic acts of violence” in the said provinces which were allegedly committed by lawless groups.

UPB Freshies nakiisa sa mga kilos-protesta
// Kate Paulyn Tayco

Nasubok ang pakikiisa ng 642 na mga bagong iskolar ng bayan ng Unibersidad ng Pilipinas Baguio (UPB) sa pagtindig laban sa mga isyung kinahaharap ng bayan sa sunod-sunod na mga kilos-protesta sa kanilang unang linggo sa unibersidad.

Sa naganap na Freshman Welcome Orientation sa bagong gawang Himnasyo Amianan noong ika-5 ng Agosto, pinangunahan ni Nationalist Corps Head Perine Agraviador ang lightning rally kung saan hinamon niya ang mga freshman na tumindig para sa bayan.

Ilan sa mga itinampok na isyu ni Agraviador ay ang problema sa edukasyon tulad na lamang ng Commission on Higher Education (CHED) Memorandum Order No. 20 o ang pag-alis ng wika at panitikan bilang core courses sa kolehiyo.

Nabigyang diin din ang Memorandum Order No. 32 na naglalayong magtalaga ng mga dagdag na tauhan ng Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP) sa Bicol, Samar, at Isla ng Negros upang bawasan ang bilang ng mga karahasan na di umano ay gawa ng mga “lawless groups.”

Kaugnay nito, naging malakas ang panawagan ng mga progresibong grupo at Konseho ng mag-aaral sa UPB sa mga bagong iskolar na makiisa sa mga kampanya laban sa mga isyu na kinahaharap ng masa.

Naglunsad din ng kilos-protesta sa kantina ng UPB sa unang araw ng mga bagong iskolar ng bayan bilang pakikiisa sa malawakang First Day Fight sa buong UP System upang sila ay himukin sa paglaban sa mga isyung kinakaharap ng bansa.

Dagdag pa rito, naging mainit din ang pagsalubong sa unang linggo ng mga bagong iskolar ng bayan sa UPB matapos ang insidente ng pagbaril sa paralegal volunteer ng Cordillera Human Rights Alliance (CHRA) at Provincial Human Rights Officer ng Ifugao na si Brandon Lee noong ika-6 ng Agosto.

Kaugnay nito, nag-organisa ng Indignation Rally ang mga progresibong grupo sa Cordillera upang kondenahin ang naging pag-atake kay Lee sa sarili nilang bahay.

“Sa una, kinilabutan ako dahil unang beses kong makakita ng mga kilos-protesta na laging binabalita ng mga midya pero simula ng dumalo ako sa EDs [Educational Discussions] at iba pa, doon ko nalaman na may mga ipinaglalaban pala ang UPB para sa masa na sa tingin ko ay ipaglalaban ko rin bilang bagong iskolar,” pahayag ni Bryan Dominic Agor, isa sa mga bagong iskolar ng bayan mula sa Kolehiyo ng Agham Panlipunan.

Piston Baguio-Benguet up to march against PUVMP
// Kricel Marianne Grace Garcillan

Baguio jeepney drivers strengthen their fight against Public Utility Vehicle Modernization program (PUVMP) after the Department of Transportation Cordillera (DOTr Car) confirms that the program will push through, August 23.

Pinagkaisang Samahan ng mga Tsuper at Opereytor Nationwide  (Piston) Baguio-Benguet organizes meetings with jeepney drivers from different areas to talk about their propose action against PUVMP and to unify their voices in disagreement to the program. They also held a dialogue with government officials to extend their contentions.

“We, in the transport sector, are against PUVMP because it is stated under the guidelines that our individual franchise would be revoked in which small operators and drivers will be at disadvantage,” said Lito Wayas, Piston Baguio-Benguet, President.

Moreover, Wayas explained that the government requires them to form cooperatives wherein, they would have one route, one operator and one franchise. As such, an operator or independent jeepney drivers would have to consolidate to form one fleet which would cause the eventual revocation of their individual franchise.

“The problem with forming cooperatives is that it is an onerous thing to do and is costly specially to small operators. And even if we consolidate to form one fleet the cost per unit of a modern jeep which is about 1.6-2.4 million pesos is still inaccessible for us,” Wayas added.

Meanwhile, Piston Baguio-Benguet already passed a position paper to DOTr Car highlighting their sentiments on the program and suggesting a more feasible solution considering their financial capabilities on pursuing the goal of PUVMP.

Wayas said that on their position paper they proposed to rehabilitate jeeps to be road worthy as defined under PUVMP instead of completely scrapping old jeepneys because buying new ones would be more expensive. He also emphasized that the government should also consider the terrain of Baguio since the new vehicles are not made for steep roads.

Furthermore, disadvantages of PUVMP will not just be felt by the transport sector but also by regular commuters as operators are required to have a minimum of 15 modern jeep units and considering the price of each units means that they will have to regain their investments by increasing the minimum fare.

PUVMP is a flagship program of the Duterte Administration which aims to provide a modern, well-managed and environmentally sustainable transport sector. It includes, modernized public utility vehicles and sets of new guidelines stated in a web post by Land Transportation Franchising and Regulatory Board.

However, the transport sector strictly opposes the program deeming it as anti-poor development project thus Piston will be holding a nationwide united transport protest action on September 30 as a response to the approval of PUVMP.

Baguio Youth speak in SOYA 2019
// Adrianne Paul Aniban

Youth leaders from different organizations in Baguio City gathered together for the State of the Youth Address (SOYA) 2019, titled Youthspeak: Youth Voice Matters, at the UPB Outcrop Office on July 20.

Richard Gabaen of PIGSA Cordillera discussed the current youth situation in Metro Baguio, mentioning the issues faced by the youth such as neoliberal attacks on education, which manifests in the declining rate of enrollment, budget cuts and lack of accessibility to students. Gabaen said that not all students can be catered by State Universities and Colleges which then force them to enroll in Private Higher Education Institutions (PHEIs).

“Besides having to pay tuition fees, students from PHEIs also experience annual tuition and other fees increase and carry over schemes,” he said.

The delegates, comprising a majority of K-12 graduates also shared their insights on the implementation of the K-12 curriculum, which was said to be unprepared, rushed and ineffective.

Gabaen also stated that the student’s democratic rights are under attack.

“Student’s right to protest and mobilize are being repressed, student publications face censorship and academic standing of students in the university is threatened,“ he said.

Furthermore, he mentioned about the attacks on youths such as political vilification and youth militarization through Mandatory ROTC. Gabaen also discussed the situation of Indigenous youths in Baguio, in which, he stated that the majority of them are currently in diaspora and that many of them have grown out of their cultural heritage.

In line with SOYA, the youth leaders also held the 2019 Baguio-Benguet Youth Covenant followed by a breakout session to discuss the issues of each participating schools in Baguio.

The event was organized by the Cordilleran Youth Center (CYC), College Editors Guild of the Philippines – Cordillera (CEGP-Cordi), National Union of Students of the Philippines Cordillera (NUSP-Cordi), Rise for Education Alliance Baguio-Benguet (R4E-BB), Progressive Igorots for Social Action (PIGSA), and Kabataan Partylist Cordillera (KPL-Cordi).
